PA_graphs_check

Parameters to evaluate:
- presence-absence ratio
- interest species inclusion
- according to the species abundance

Scenarios:

  • Niche form: “noeffect”, “concavebell”,“convexbell”, “possigmoid”, “negsigmoid”
  • Species abundance: “rare”, “abundant”
  • Absence = “*1”, “max/2”, “max”
  • includeSP: T, F (with or without interest sp)

Data simulation:

We simulate presence-absence data for each niche form and for each species abundance (rare, abundant) according to environmental value (transmittance). We keep all the presences, and sampled a number of absences according to the scenario (*1, max/2, max ), including or not the species of interest in the absences (includeSP).

Evaluation criteria:
- presence distribution
- niche form
- close optimum

Check pseudo-absences tests graphicaly:
- Check presence: Presence density plot - obs vs est
- Check niche form: Proba facet - obs vs est
- Check optimum : Extremum plot

Color code:
- turquoise: simulated data
- black: estimated data by bayesian inference

plist <- vector('list', length(unique(data$Scenario)))

for(p in 1:length(unique(data$Scenario))){
  plist[[p]] <- local({
    
    dataS <- data %>% 
      filter(Scenario==unique(data$Scenario)[p]) %>% 
      mutate(mumax = max(mu, na.rm = T)) %>% 
      mutate(TotProbabilitymax = max(TotProbability)) %>% 
      mutate(TotProbability_rescale = ifelse(TotProbabilitymax > mumax,
                                             TotProbability/(TotProbabilitymax/mumax), TotProbability)) %>%
      
      mutate(mu_rescale = ifelse(!is.na(mu) & TotProbabilitymax < mumax,
                                 mu/(mumax/TotProbabilitymax), mu)) %>% 
      
      mutate(`5%_rescale` = ifelse(!is.na(`5%`) & TotProbabilitymax < mumax,
                                   `5%`/(mumax/TotProbabilitymax), `5%`)) %>% 
      
      mutate(`95%_rescale` = ifelse(!is.na(`95%`) & TotProbabilitymax < mumax,
                                    `95%`/(mumax/TotProbabilitymax), `95%`))
    
    ggplot(dataS, aes(x= logTransmittance, y= Presence)) +
      xlim(min(data$logTransmittance), 0) +
      
      # Total

      # Probas estimated
      geom_point(aes(y = mu_rescale), size=0.7) +
      geom_ribbon(aes(ymin = `5%_rescale`, ymax = `95%_rescale`), na.rm = T, color = 'red', alpha = 0.2) +
      
      labs(title = unique(dataS$Scenario), y="Presence probability rescaled") +
      
      theme_minimal()
    
  })

Results

Concave:

  • Only the rare−max−TRUE scenario doesn’t include the simulated optimum in the estimated optimum at 95% confidence interval. - The scenarios that include the species of interest in absences have the estimated median optimum furthest from the simulated optimum, for both rare and abundant species.
  • When the species of interest is not included in the absences, whatever the abundance of the species, the estimated median optimum is closest to the simulated optimum in the case of a balanced presence-absence ratio, followed closely by the other two scenarios (max/2 and max). However, in the case of the balanced scenario, the estimated distribution of the optimum is wider and therefore more uncertain than in the scenarios with more absences (max/2 and max).
  • The estimated optimum uncertainty is smaller for the abundant species than for the rare species, and negligible compared to the range of variation of the environmental variable (uncertainty of 0.01 on a 0-1 range).
  • The estimated optimum uncertainty of the rare species can be up to 0.07 on a 0-1 range for the balanced scenario. At best, by excluding scenarios including the species of interest, the uncertainty is 0.05 by adding absences (max/2 and max).

Bilan

  • In all the scenarios, the estimated species niche is close to the simulated species niche.
  • The abundant species is less sensible to the different tested parameters, than the rare species.
  • Scenarios with most absences (max/2 and max) permit to estimate the niche closer to the simulated niche
  • Include the species of interest in absences shift the niche optimum
